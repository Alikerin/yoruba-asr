Jíjí Èèyàn Gbé Ti Di Òwò Tó Kárí Ayé ## Kidnapping — A Global Business
Ọ̀RÀN jíjí èèyàn gbé ṣẹlẹ̀ gan - an káàkiri ayé láàárín ọdún mẹ́wàá tó kọjá . ## DURING the past decade , a remarkable surge in kidnappings has taken place worldwide .
Ìròyìn kan sọ pé láàárín ọdún 1968 sí 1982 , àwọn èèyàn tí iye wọ́n fẹ́rẹ̀ẹ́ tó ẹgbẹ̀rún kan láti orílẹ̀ - èdè mẹ́tàléláàádọ́rin ní a mú ní òǹdè . ## One report says that between 1968 and 1982 , nearly a thousand hostages were taken in 73 countries .
Ṣùgbọ́n ní nǹkan bí ọdún 1997 sí 1999 , nǹkan bí ọ̀kẹ́ kan [ 20,000 ] sí ọ̀kẹ́ kan ààbọ̀ [ 30,000 ] èèyàn ni wọ́n ń jí gbé lọ́dọọdún . ## But in the late 1990 ’ s , an estimated 20,000 to 30,000 people were kidnapped each year .
Ó jọ pé òwò jíjí èèyàn gbé ló lòde báyìí láàárín àwọn ọ̀daràn lórígun mẹ́rẹ̀ẹ̀rin àgbáyé láti Rọ́ṣíà títí dé Philippines , tí kò sì sí ẹ̀dá táwọn ajínigbé ò lè jí gbé . ## Kidnapping is a crime that seems to be in fashion among criminals from Russia to the Philippines , with kidnappers ready to grab at anything that moves .
Nínú ìṣẹ̀lẹ̀ kan , wọ́n jí ọmọ jòjòló tí kò tíì lò tó ọjọ́ kan láyé gbé . ## On one occasion a baby barely one day old was kidnapped .

Nígbà kan , àwọn olówó làwọn tó ń jí èèyàn gbé ń ṣọdẹ kiri , ṣùgbọ́n ìlù ti yí padà báyìí . ## In the past , kidnappers targeted principally the rich , but times have changed .
